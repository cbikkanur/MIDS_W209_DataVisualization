I was never a bibliophile until 2016, when I read books authored by Dale Carnegie, Napoleon Hill and Robert Kiyosaki. I started reading more non-fiction books as I found them more useful and applicable to real life scenarios. In 2019, I have taken up a challenge (goodreads.com challenge) to read 25 books by the end of the year. But, I read 24 books in 2019 and thus failing the challenge by 1 book. In above visualizations, you can see there is a spike in 2019 for the number of books I read.
  • In visualization 1, you can mouseover the data point to see more details about each book such as title, author, rating and the date I completed reading it.
  • In visualization 2, you can get the insights into how many books I read in each month over the years.
  • In visualization 3, you can choose and sort the books by 1) Number of Pages 2) GoodRead's Rating for the book.
  • In visualization 4, you can get a glimpse of total number books I have read so far and the number of books I am currently reading and will be reading.
